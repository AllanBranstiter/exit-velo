The best pitcher the Reds will see all weekend, and arguably the best in the American League right now. Ryan works primarily with a four-seam fastball that generates swings and misses, a wipeout slider, and a knuckle-curve. He has been getting hitters to chase pitches out of the zone at an elite rate, and the gap between his actual ERA and his expected numbers suggests he has been slightly unlucky. FanGraphs recently featured an article about his Twins teammate, but Ryan has quietly been the better pitcher. The Reds lineup will need patience and early-count aggressiveness to avoid falling behind.

The most hittable of the three Twins starters this weekend. Ober is a tall, lanky right-hander who relies on deception and extension rather than velocity, throwing in the upper 80s.
