Lowder is not a power pitcher. He wins by hitting his spots, changing speeds, and getting hitters to swing at pitches they cannot handle. Think of him as a craftsman rather than a flamethrower. What makes him dangerous is that hitters know he is not going to overpower them, yet they keep chasing anyway, and that is a real skill. The key thing to watch: his margin for error is smaller than a hard thrower's. When he is painting corners, he looks like a number-two starter. When he is slightly off, hitters will make him pay quickly.

Data sources: 2024–2026 Statcast via FanGraphs (Pitching+, Stuff+, Location+). Start projections from Razzball. Stats through April 5, 2026.

Expected ERA: ERA estimate based on contact quality allowed, not results. A better predictor than ERA itself.
FIP: ERA based only on strikeouts, walks, and home runs. Strips out defense and luck.
K%-BB%: Strikeout rate minus walk rate. The single best command efficiency number.
Hard-Hit Rate: % of balls in play hit 95+ mph. Higher is worse for the pitcher.
Exit Velocity: Average speed off the bat for balls put in play. Higher is worse for the pitcher.
Chase Rate: How often batters swing at pitches outside the zone. Higher is better for the pitcher.
Ground Ball Rate: % of balls in play that stay on the ground. Ground balls rarely leave the park.
Stuff+ / Location+ / Pitching+: Pitch quality grades. 100 = league average. Above 100 = above average.
Slider Pitching+: Pitching+ grade specifically for the slider. Above 100 = above-average slider.
